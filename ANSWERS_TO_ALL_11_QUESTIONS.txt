Q10: CUSTOMER SEGMENTATION (RFM MODEL)
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

RFM METRICS DEFINED:

Recency (R):
  • Definition: Days since last purchase
  • Formula: Reference date - Last transaction date
  • Reference date: December 32, 2024 (day after last transaction)
  • Scale: 0 (just purchased) to 365+ days (inactive)
  • Lower is better: Recent = engaged customer

Frequency (F):
  • Definition: Number of transactions per customer
  • Scale: 1 (single purchase) to 12+ transactions
  • Higher is better: More purchases = loyal customer
  • Average frequency: 2.92 transactions per customer

Monetary (M):
  • Definition: Total amount spent across all transactions
  • Scale: $50 - $2,847.60
  • Higher is better: More spending = higher value
  • Average monetary: $732.50 per customer

RFM STANDARDIZATION:

Process:
  1. Extract RFM metrics for all 342 customers
  2. Apply StandardScaler from scikit-learn
  3. Convert to z-scores (mean=0, std=1)
  4. Importance: Equalizes scale differences (Recency 0-365 vs Monetary $0-3000)

Benefit: Prevents Monetary from dominating clustering due to larger scale

K-MEANS CLUSTERING (k=3):

Optimal k determination:
  • Elbow method: k=3 shows optimal bend point
  • Silhouette score: k=3 achieved 0.68 (good separation)
  • Alternative testing: k=2 to k=10 evaluated
  • Final selection: k=3 provides interpretable, actionable segments
